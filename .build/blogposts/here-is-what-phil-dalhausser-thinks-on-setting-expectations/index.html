Here is what Phil Dalhausser thinks on setting expectations

"I always expect to win" says Phil Dalhausser in this podcast. Learn from Phil about setting expectations, making plans with your partner and the effect of expectations on the score board

Short resume:

  • Expect to win. The moment you settle for 3rd place mentally, your brain subsconsiously stops you from even trying harder and chasing those hard balls

  • Self confidence is what determines many time the outcome. The technique can be perfect and the injury recovery might be complete but if you don't believe you'll win - you never will

  • Use a 3-tier goals system

  • Eliminate things preventing your progress (such as everyday parties) and proactively seek opportunities for improvement (such as moving to a place with better volley infrastructure and players)

  • Move the legs, says Dalhausser, reminding us that volleyball is a leg game

  • When blocking look at the attacker, don't make the mistake of looking just at the ball

  • Consistency, consistency, consistency in your technique, in your breakfast, in your warmup will keep you at the top for a long run

  • Now, when the prematch expectations are set, throw them completely out of you head during the match. Focus on the task at hand. Phil suggests you think about how would you react to this or that type of server and never second guess yourself unless you want to put a ball in the net
