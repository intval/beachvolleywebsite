Fixing jumpers knee

Stopping that annoying pain directly under or above the knee cap is easier than you think. It doesn't take $$$ spent on physiotherapy or any weird equipment. In just two weeks my struggle with knee pain vanished - and never came back.

Before you try anything - visit a doctor

Warning:

Curing the wrong problem will not help you much. More likely it will introduce a second problem, which you definitely don't want. Consult with medical personel first!

No pain no gain

This specific type of pain is a result of body growth. Is it good than? Far from that.

Your body is an amazing device. It has bones as structure and muscles to move one bone relative to another. A bunch of special fibers called tendons connect the muscles to the bones.

When you decide to contract you muscles the tendons get as much stress as the muscles themselves. Normally there is a limit on how much force a muscle can produce and your visits to the gym push that limit upwards. The body adapts and the muscle grows. So is the tendon, which adapts to support the bigger force and the stronger muscle.

But here is the problem. The muscle grows significantly faster than the tendon, and constant preasure on the muscles makes it stronger, leaving the tendon lagging behind, unable to provide the necessary support for it.

A set of special exercises for the tendons will address just that. Two weeks of 3x15 repetitions, twice a day and you will be back on your athletic track. I've seen the pain from walking up the stairs completely gone away even after multiple hours training and playing session.

Single-leg decline board squat

Research suggests that a very effective excercise for curing patellar tendonitis is the decline board single leg squat. This type of exercise puts more preasure on the tendon than on the muscle, causing it to grow and become stronger.

All you need is a board with about 25 degrees angle. You can easily make a board like this yourself, taking a wooden peace and putting one side of it on an elevated surface. For progress, you may squat with additional weight in hands or, following Martin Koban's book suggestion, slightly rotate your body.

Remember, the focus is the descent. Count to five on your way down and use the second leg to assist standing up.
